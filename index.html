Life Before Relias (and development)

I worked as a speech-language pathologist with acutely ill adult patients. I spent most of my career working at Duke Hospital on the cardiothoracic surgical unit. While I did provide what people traditionally think of as speech therapy -- helping those who cannot communicate due to a stroke or other impairment, most of my caseload were patients who were unable to eat and drink due to surgical complications. You can see one of the eating and drinking studies we complete above, passing a tiny scope via a nostril to take a look at the upper airway.

I had the opportunity to complete performance improvement projects and present and state and national conferences. Here you can see a poster from the national conference this year, where we presented our findings on implementing a dextrose-filled capsule during our fiberoptic swallowing study.
